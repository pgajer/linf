\section*{Supplementary Methods}

Outcome flags in the AGP discovery cohort were derived directly from the
\texttt{Phenotype} metadata field by fixed substring matching. The focal
outcomes used in the main manuscript were IBS (\texttt{irritable bowel
syndrome}), IBD (\texttt{inflammatory bowel disease}), autoimmune disease
(\texttt{autoimmune}), acid reflux (\texttt{acid reflux}), and seasonal
allergies (\texttt{seasonal allergies}). Samples without the focal phrase
served as controls for that outcome. These outcome flags were therefore
not mutually exclusive across the discovery cohort.

dCST construction was applied after the discovery feature-retention
filter described in the main manuscript. The labels should therefore be
read as retained-feature dominance states, not as claims about every raw
feature in the original table. Feature names use the deepest informative
SILVA annotation available for each retained feature, which is why the
tables include a mixture of species, genus, family, order, and unresolved
labels.

The AGP discovery phenotype labels are self-reported and do not provide a
uniform treatment-naive clinical IBD classification. IBD subtype, disease
activity, medication exposure, and recent antibiotic exposure were not
consistently available for adjusted discovery modeling. The AGP
associations are therefore interpreted as phenotype-screening results and
are paired with external validation rather than treated as clinical
mechanistic estimates on their own.

The adjusted frequentist label-level analyses used logistic regression
with age, sex, and BMI as covariates. The adjusted Bayesian follow-up
used \texttt{arm::bayesglm} with the same covariate structure and the
package's default weakly informative prior. For each label we summarize
the adjusted Bayesian fit by the exponentiated coefficient estimate, an
approximate 95\% credible interval based on the posterior standard
error, and the posterior probability that the label coefficient exceeds
0. Unadjusted Bayesian prevalence contrasts were estimated separately
with a beta-binomial model using a Jeffreys prior,
\(\mathrm{Beta}(0.5,0.5)\).

External validation was evaluated in two complementary modes. In the
rebuilt-cohort mode, each external count matrix was filtered and
analyzed independently with the same absorb workflow used for the AGP
discovery cohort. In the frozen-transfer mode, external samples were
mapped directly onto the AGP-derived absorb hierarchy after aligning the
external taxa to the AGP retained-feature dictionary. The first mode
tests framework reproducibility; the second tests portability of the
AGP-defined label dictionary itself.

\section*{Supplementary Results}

\noindent\textbf{Acid reflux.} Acid reflux was the clearest additional
non-IBD gastrointestinal branch in the AGP screen. At depth 1 it was
associated with enrichment of \taxon{Bacteroides} (adjusted OR 1.28, q
= 1.75e-05) together with depletion of \taxon{Streptococcus} and
\taxon{Staphylococcus}. The signal then sharpened into deeper
\taxon{Bacteroides}-centered lineages enriched for
\taxon{Parabacteroides} and \taxon{Alistipes}, including
\taxon{Bacteroides}\dcstsep\taxon{Parabacteroides}\dcstsep Lachnospiraceae
(adjusted OR 2.64, q = 0.00419). Bayesian follow-up retained support
for numerous depth-4 descendants after the frequentist signal thinned.
Because reflux symptoms are self-reported and likely influenced by diet,
medication, and upper-gastrointestinal factors, we regard this branch as
hypothesis-generating rather than disease-specific.

\medskip

\noindent\textbf{Seasonal allergies.} Seasonal allergies provided a
complementary immune-linked example outside the core inflammatory
phenotypes. The dominant pattern was depletion of
\taxon{Prevotella\_9}-linked states together with enrichment of
enterobacterial lineages. At depth 1,
\taxon{Prevotella\_9} was depleted (adjusted OR 0.67, q = 2.77e-08),
whereas \taxon{Escherichia-Shigella} was modestly enriched. At depth 2,
the strongest enriched state was
\taxon{Escherichia-Shigella}\dcstsep\taxon{Bacteroides} (adjusted OR
1.24, q = 7.42e-06), while
\taxon{Prevotella\_9}\dcstsep\taxon{Bacteroides} was depleted (adjusted
OR 0.64, q = 0.000215). Bayesian follow-up preserved this paired
pattern at greater depth, but we interpret the branch as exploratory
rather than as a fully validated disease axis.
