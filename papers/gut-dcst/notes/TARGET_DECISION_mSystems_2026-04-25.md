# Target Decision: mSystems

Date: 2026-04-25

## Decision

The recommended first submission target for the current gut dCST manuscript is
`mSystems`.

## Why mSystems is the better fit right now

The current manuscript is strongest as:

- a systems-microbiology paper with a clear gut application;
- a deterministic representation framework paper rather than a mechanistic
  causality paper;
- a cross-cohort portability and interpretability paper rather than a
  multi-omics integration paper.

That profile matches `mSystems` better than `Microbiome` at the current project
state.

## Why not Microbiome first

`Microbiome` explicitly says it is especially interested in studies that go
beyond descriptive omics surveys and include mechanistic support for proposed
functions and, if possible, cause-and-effect relationships. That makes the
current paper a stretch fit, because our strongest contribution is portable,
deterministic state representation plus external validation, not mechanism or
causality.

## Why mSystems fits

ASM currently describes `mSystems` as a multidisciplinary open-access journal
publishing preeminent work in microbial systems biology, including important
biological and biochemical findings drawn from analyses of large data sets, as
well as new computational approaches that yield insights into microbial systems
at the scale of both the single cell and microbial communities.

That is much closer to what this paper now does:

- learns a deterministic state hierarchy from a large ecological cohort;
- transports that hierarchy into external cohorts;
- tests where portability holds and where it breaks;
- emphasizes reusable state vocabulary, interpretability, and reproducibility.

## Practical implication for framing

For `mSystems`, the paper should present itself as:

- a deterministic dominant-community state framework;
- a gut-microbiome application showing cross-cohort transport and limits;
- a useful analysis layer for future clinically curated and multi-omics work.

It should not present itself as:

- a clinical biomarker paper;
- a mechanistic causality paper;
- or a final disease-stratification endpoint.

## Working submission stance

The recommended editorial stance is:

1. submit to `mSystems` first;
2. keep the paper centered on representation, transferability, and validation;
3. treat this manuscript as the first step before the next gflow and
   multi-omics round.
