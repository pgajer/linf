# Reviewer Suggestions for mSystems Submission

Date: 2026-04-25

## Notes

- These suggestions are aimed at `mSystems`, where the paper should be read as a
  systems-microbiology and microbiome-analysis manuscript rather than as a
  biomarker paper.
- Before submission, please do one final conflict check for recent
  collaborations, co-authorship, mentoring relationships, or other conflicts
  involving either author.
- I would avoid suggesting investigators whose cohorts are central source data
  for this paper if that relationship feels too close to a data-owner conflict.

## Primary Suggestions

### 1. Jeroen Raes

- Affiliation: KU Leuven and VIB-KU Leuven Center for Microbiology, Belgium
- Email: `jeroen.raes@kuleuven.be`
- Why this is a good fit:
  - internationally recognized for gut microbial ecology, enterotypes, and
    cross-cohort microbiome analysis;
  - strong systems and meta-omics perspective;
  - well positioned to assess whether dCSTs add something meaningful beyond
    clustering-defined community types.

### 2. Georg Zeller

- Affiliation: EMBL Heidelberg, Germany
- Email: `georg.zeller@embl.de`
- Why this is a good fit:
  - expert in computational analysis of host-microbiota interactions in disease;
  - strong experience with microbiome phenotype-association modeling and
    cross-cohort analysis;
  - well suited to assess the transferability and limitations of the AGP-derived
    state vocabulary.

### 3. Nicola Segata

- Affiliation: CIBIO, University of Trento, Italy
- Email: `nicola.segata@unitn.it`
- Why this is a good fit:
  - leading figure in computational metagenomics and human microbiome profiling;
  - deep expertise in microbiome bioinformatics tools and cross-study
    interpretability;
  - well suited to evaluate the methodological contribution and portability
    claims.

### 4. Falk Hildebrand

- Affiliation: Quadram Institute Bioscience and Earlham Institute, United Kingdom
- Email: `falk.hildebrand@quadram.ac.uk`
- Why this is a good fit:
  - strong expertise in gut microbiome ecology, metagenomics, and interpretable
    community-structure analysis;
  - relevant experience with enterosignatures and human gut state-space
    concepts;
  - well positioned to assess the ecological meaning of the dCST hierarchy.

### 5. Patrick D. Schloss

- Affiliation: University of Michigan, United States
- Email: `pschloss@umich.edu`
- Why this is a good fit:
  - expert in computational microbial ecology, microbiome methodology, and
    reproducibility;
  - especially strong reviewer for deterministic state-typing claims,
    benchmarking logic, and analysis transparency;
  - helpful counterweight to more clinically focused reviewers.

### 6. Harry Sokol

- Affiliation: AP-HP / Sorbonne Université / INSERM, France
- Email: `harry.sokol@aphp.fr`
- Why this is a good fit:
  - IBD clinician-scientist with strong microbiome expertise;
  - well suited to evaluate the CD/UC framing, Crohn-location interpretation,
    and the clinical plausibility of the external validation results;
  - helpful for ensuring the paper reads as biologically credible without
    overstating its clinical claims.
