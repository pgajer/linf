## Role of the gut application in the new paper

The gut application should carry the paper's:

- portability claim
- shared-taxonomy harmonization claim
- external validation logic
- subject-aware realism about repeated sampling

It should \textbf{not} try to carry the entire paper by itself.

## Main-text subsection flow

## 3.2.1 AGP hierarchy and exploratory gut prioritization

### Keep

- local-QZA SILVA AGP structure-learning cohort summary
- brief justification for AGP as ecological background rather than diagnosis-grade source
- concise statement that exploratory AGP screening prioritized self-reported IBD for follow-up

### Compress

- detailed non-IBD phenotype side branches
- long AGP condition-screen narrative

### Main figure support

- the gut overview figure should show the AGP screening result in a compact way

## 3.2.2 External transfer to Halfvarson as the main stool-based test

### Keep

- Halfvarson as the clearest stool-based external validation cohort
- sample-level significance in pooled IBD, Crohn disease, and ulcerative colitis
- the strongest transferred taxonomic states
- rebuilt-versus-transfer contrast

### Must say clearly

- sample-level significance remains real
- one-sample-per-subject sensitivity weakens the direct disease-versus-control transfer signal
- this is a portability result with realistic longitudinal caveats, not a diagnostic-biomarker claim

### Main figure support

- the combined-paper gut figure should still have a Figure 2B-like summary layer

## 3.2.3 HMP2 and Gevers as portability boundaries

### Keep

- HMP2 current slice as a mucosal-only complement
- Gevers as a pediatric Crohn-focused boundary case
- clear statement that these are not positive transfer replications in the current analysis

### Compress

- overly fine details that were useful only in the gut-only manuscript

### Purpose

- these cohorts help define the limits of the framework rather than just pad the paper

## 3.2.4 One within-disease extension in main text

### Preferred choice

- keep Crohn location as the one main-text within-disease follow-up

### Why

- it is the strongest and most interpretable one
- it connects to known ileal-versus-colonic biology
- it shows that rebuilt and transferred modes are not interchangeable

### Move to supplement or compress heavily

- UC extent
- calprotectin threshold details
- extended sensitivity layers that do not sharpen the main framework claim

## 3.2.5 Gut takeaway paragraph

This should close the gut Results section and make the transition to CT easier.

### It should state

- dCSTs can support external taxonomic state transfer in gut microbiome data
- portability is real but not universal
- subject-aware sensitivity matters
- this motivates a second application that tests the framework in a different niche and analysis regime

## What to leave out of main text

- most of the AGP exploratory phenotype supplement material
- extended direct-transfer audit detail
- thresholded calprotectin detours
- repetitive wording distinguishing every reviewer-driven sensitivity layer
